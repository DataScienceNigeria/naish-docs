Deployment covers everything from importing the hardware to formal acceptance
and handover of a running node. The figures below are drawn from the
UduTech–Kasi RFP response and describe the vendor's proposed delivery approach.

## Vendor / consortium

The **UduTech–Kasi Cloud Consortium** delivers the node under two complementary
roles:

- **UduTech** – technical advisory and orchestration (platform enablement,
  scheduler integration, multi-user setup).
- **Kasi Cloud** – Supermicro partner responsible for importation, logistics,
  and on-ground engineering (physical delivery, racking, and commissioning at
  GBB).

Technical sign-off authority rests with **NCAIR** per the RFP.

## Delivery timeline

The consortium proposes a phased schedule from award to handover. On award, a
**3-day mobilisation to GBB** begins.

| Phase | Duration |
| --- | --- |
| Hardware imported and delivered to Lagos | 12–20 weeks |
| Transit from Lagos to GBB, Abuja | 3–7 days |
| Installation, commissioning, and acceptance | 7–14 days |
| Software provisioning and load testing | 7–14 days |
| Training, acceptance sign-off, and handover | a few days |
| **Total (award → handover)** | **~16–26 weeks** |

The long pole is hardware importation; once on site, the node moves through
commissioning, software provisioning, and acceptance relatively quickly.

## Acceptance test plan

Acceptance (RFP §7) is a structured sequence of tests, each of which must pass
before the node is considered delivered. Every stage is a formal gate rather
than an informal check.

1. **Hardware delivery acceptance** – physical inventory and inspection against
   the bill of materials.
2. **GPU benchmark** – ResNet50 plus transformer micro-benchmarks; must reach
   **≥90% of the vendor baseline** with **no thermal throttling** observed.
3. **CPU throughput** – a 60-minute sustained load test across the dual-Xeon
   platform.
4. **End-to-end pipeline** – a full ingest → preprocess → train → infer run
   completed within the agreed SLA.
5. **Power / thermal stress** – a 2-hour sustained draw plus a UPS-switchover
   test, confirming the site holds the node's peak load.
6. **Network / cross-site orchestration** – validation of the 200 GbE fabric and
   any cross-site coordination.
7. **Scheduler integration** – Slurm / Kubernetes / AGH scheduling with
   preemption behaviour verified.
8. **Telemetry** – IPMI / Redfish / Prometheus telemetry streams confirmed live
   and complete.

<Callout type="info">
  The power / thermal stress test is directly coupled to GBB physical readiness
  for the full **10.2 kW peak draw** – a live dependency tracked as remediation
  findings R1–R4. See [Governance & Risk](/docs/delivery/governance-risk).
</Callout>

## Warranty & support

The support model combines OEM and local coverage so the node stays serviceable
in-country.

- **Warranty:** 36-month OEM warranty plus a local vendor warranty.
- **Local spares buffer:** DIMMs, drives, PSUs, fans, and NIC held locally to
  avoid import lead times on failures.
- **Firmware & patching:** quarterly firmware updates and monthly security
  patches.

### Service levels (SLA)

| Severity | Response | Remote diagnosis | On-site |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Sev 1 – node down | 2 hr | 4 hr | 24 hr |
| Sev 2 – degraded | 8 hr | 24 hr | 72 hr |

## Compliance claims (vendor)

The vendor states the platform is **NDPA-compliant**, with **SOC 2 / ISO 27001
mapping near completion**, and aligned with GBB physical and network security
controls. These are vendor-stated positions to be verified during acceptance.
