Governance is treated as first-class content: the node is a shared, multi-tenant
resource, and without explicit rules it cannot safely serve seven use cases plus
open startup and researcher access. This page consolidates the cross-cutting
governance items and open risks from the advisory record.

## Workload governance framework

The recurring critical dependency across the advisory series is a **workload
governance framework** – the set of rules that decides who gets GPU time, when,
and at what priority.

It must formalise, *before any use case enters production*:

- **Quota policies** – how much GPU time, memory, and storage each tenant may
  hold.
- **Priority tiers** – which workloads preempt which, and which are protected.
- **Scheduling rules** – how inference and training windows are placed so they
  do not collide.

A **formal GPU workload calendar** operationalises this: training and
fine-tuning are scheduled into off-peak inference windows so perpetual inference
services are not degraded by heavy batch jobs.

## Clinical safety gate (Self-CAIRE)

<Callout type="error" title="Non-negotiable prerequisite">
  For the Self-CAIRE digital health coach, clinical fine-tuning plus safety
  evaluation (estimated ~210 GPU-hours) is a hard pre-deployment gate. The
  service cannot enter production until RLHF / DPO alignment and safety
  evaluation are complete.
</Callout>

This is the highest-criticality use case: it gives health guidance to patients,
so a failure mode is clinical harm, not just a bad answer.

## Data readiness

For **AI POCUS** and **Health Record Digitisation**, the primary constraint is
**dataset availability, not compute**. The node can train these models faster
than usable, curated Nigerian datasets can be assembled – so data pipelines and
governance, not GPU procurement, are the pacing item.

Health-record digitisation additionally requires **phased facility onboarding**:
the full national backlog could reach **~310,000+ GPU-hours** and would exceed a
single node's capacity without phasing across facilities over time.

## GBB physical readiness

Physical hosting depends on the GBB Data Centre being ready for the node's full
load.

<Callout type="warn">
  GBB physical readiness for the full **10.2 kW peak draw** (power and cooling)
  is a live dependency. Remediation findings tracked as **R1–R4** are not yet
  confirmed complete.
</Callout>

The node also needs 2× 15 kW metered PDUs and ~1,105 CFM of airflow to reject
~38,557 BTU/hr – all of which the acceptance power / thermal stress test
validates against the site.

## Usage-baseline correction

An earlier planning figure of "2 sessions / user / month" **understated
production demand**. Planning should instead use realistic concurrency – DSN
suggests **500–1,000 concurrent sessions** for agricultural advisory at national
pilot scale as a baseline.

## Hosting options under analysis

Three hosting models are under economic analysis:

1. **Full deployment at GBB** – the whole node hosted in Abuja.
2. **Hybrid / split model** – see the constraint below.
3. **Commercial data centre** – a third-party facility.

<Callout type="error" title="Hybrid ≠ cluster">
  The hybrid model is **two independent parallel servers, not a distributed
  cluster** – a direct consequence of the [HGX single-baseboard
  constraint](/docs/infrastructure/architecture-notes). The intent is to fold
  external GPUs back into GBB once R1–R4 remediation is confirmed complete.
</Callout>

The political / mandate dimension of GBB hosting is framed diplomatically as a
**strategic alignment** consideration in external documents. A CAPEX / OPEX
economic-comparison across the three models is in progress, pending confirmed
market rates and GBB remediation inputs.

## Risk register

| Risk | Status | Mitigation |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Workload governance framework not yet formalised | Open – critical | Formalise quotas, tiers, and calendar before any production use case |
| GBB physical readiness (R1–R4) | Open | Remediation tracked; validated by power / thermal acceptance test |
| Clinical safety (Self-CAIRE) | Gated | Fine-tuning + safety evaluation required before deployment |
| Dataset availability (POCUS, Health Records) | Open | Data pipelines and phased onboarding, not more compute |
| HGX single-baseboard limits distribution | Structural | Hybrid = two parallel servers, not a cluster |
