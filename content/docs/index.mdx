The **Nigeria AI Scaling Hub (NAISH)** is a national initiative to deploy and
operate a sovereign, high-performance GPU compute node as a shared, governed
resource – giving Nigerian startups, researchers, and public-sector teams access
to the kind of compute that AI at national scale demands, hosted on national
infrastructure at the **Galaxy Backbone Data Centre in Abuja**.

NAISH was publicly launched in **June 2026** by the **Federal Ministry of
Communications, Innovation & Digital Economy (FMCIDE)** in collaboration with the
**Gates Foundation** – spanning technical assistance, computing infrastructure, policy
support, and strategic partnerships. Its purpose is to move high-impact AI
solutions **beyond pilots to nationwide deployment** across health, agriculture,
and education, and to position Nigeria as an exemplar for responsible AI adoption
in Africa.

This documentation is the technical reference behind that mission. Everything a
builder, partner, or reviewer needs to understand the Hub lives here – the
infrastructure and hardware, the Hub's technical capabilities, the identified
use-case portfolio, node capacity, deployment and acceptance, governance and
risk, and the body of Gates-funded work the Hub builds on. Start with the
sections below.
