NAISH is a multi-stakeholder initiative, and its structure matters: no single
organisation owns every part. Government sets direction and provides the host
facility; a philanthropic funder backs the programme; **Lagos Business School, as
primary grant recipient, manages the Hub and runs its activities**; a technical
implementation partner designs and validates the infrastructure; a vendor
supplies and installs the hardware; and a set of implementers build the use cases
that run on it. This page maps who does what.

## Programme leadership & funding

| Entity / Person | Role |
| --- | --- |
| **Lagos Business School (LBS)** | Pan-Atlantic University; **primary recipient** of the Gates Foundation grant – manages the Hub and runs its activities |
| **Gates Foundation** | Programme funder |
| **FMCIDE** | Federal Ministry of Communications, Innovation & Digital Economy – sponsoring ministry, policy partner |
| **The Nigerian Public Service** | Principal recipients of validated public-sector led innovative AI solutions |
| **AI Solutions Innovators** | Developer of AI-powered solutions targeted at validated government challenges across Health, Agriculture, Education, Financial Services and Public Services |

## Infrastructure & delivery

| Entity / Person | Role |
| --- | --- |
| **Galaxy Backbone (GBB)** | Physical hosting provider – power, cooling, network – Abuja |
| **UduTech–Kasi Cloud Consortium** | GPU hardware vendor; UduTech leads technical advisory / orchestration, Kasi Cloud handles importation, logistics, and on-ground engineering |

## Use-case implementers

The organisations that build and run the individual use cases on the node:

| Implementer | Use case |
| --- | --- |
| **mDoc** | Self-CAIRE digital health coaching ("Kem" platform) |
| **Digital Green** | Agricultural advisory |
| **TARL / Pratham** | Oral Reading Fluency (ORF) assessment |

Beyond these named implementers, the Hub is open to Nigerian startups and
researchers more broadly, and – through the [SAID Challenge](/docs/overview/what-is-naish)
 – to teams matched with government institutions to solve public-sector problems.
