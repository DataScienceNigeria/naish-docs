The **EdoDiDA Chatbot** is a natural-language interface built on top of **DHIS2**
for the **Edo State Digital Governance and Data Management Agency (EdoDiDA)**.
Delivered by **Data Science Nigeria** with **Gates Foundation** support, it lets
non-technical government users query a DHIS2 database in plain English and get
back answers with a table, a visual breakdown, and a written explanation. It is a
concrete example of putting conversational AI on top of a national data platform
so that data actually reaches the people making decisions.

## What it does

EdoDiDA **democratises data access** for quick, real-world decision-making. Rather
than requiring dashboards or SQL, a user asks a question in natural language and
the chatbot:

- Translates the question into a query against the DHIS2 data.
- Returns the underlying **data table**.
- Produces a **visual breakdown** of the result.
- Adds a plain-language **explanation and context** – "snackable insights" that
  highlight what the numbers mean.
- Where relevant, offers **recommendations** (e.g. resource allocation).

## Example interactions

The chatbot works across sectors covered by the state's DHIS2 data.

**Ask a question, get a visual breakdown.** "Plot malaria cases by LGA" returns a
chart straight from the state's health data:

![Malaria cases by LGA, plotted by EdoDiDA](/edodida-chart.png)

**Ask for analysis, get plain-language insight.** "How is the farming population
distributed across each LGA?" returns per-LGA figures plus an explanation that
flags high, moderate, and low-participation areas to guide targeted intervention:

![EdoDiDA explanation of farming population distribution](/edodida-explain.png)

Beyond agriculture and health, it handles cross-sector questions such as teacher
allocation – returning figures per LGA plus recommendations that prioritise
high-enrolment and under-resourced areas.

## Why it matters

- **Data democratisation** – turns a specialist data platform (DHIS2) into
  something any policymaker can interrogate conversationally.
- **Decision support** – pairs raw figures with interpretation and
  recommendations, closing the gap between data and action.
- **Grounded answers** – responses are drawn from the government's own DHIS2
  records, not generic knowledge.

## Relevance to NAISH

EdoDiDA is a template for **conversational access to government Digital Public
Infrastructure**. DHIS2 is the same platform the Hub's
[health-record digitisation](/docs/projects-archive/identified-use-cases) use
case targets, and the natural-language-over-DHIS2 pattern generalises to other
national datasets. It exercises the Hub's
[data readiness](/docs/capabilities/data-readiness) and
[model lifecycle](/docs/capabilities/model-testing) capabilities, and – run on
sovereign compute – keeps sensitive state data in-country.
