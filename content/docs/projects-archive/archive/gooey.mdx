**Gooey.AI** is a **low-code AI orchestration platform** for building and
deploying multilingual AI "copilots" for frontline workers and underserved
populations in the Global South. Teams compose AI workflows ("recipes") from
LLM prompts, knowledge-base files, code, and evaluation datasets, then deploy
them with one click to **WhatsApp, voice/IVR, SMS, and web**. It is
model-agnostic (Google, OpenAI, Meta, Mistral, DeepSeek from one account) and
markets support for **1000+ languages**. It is included in this archive because
its deployment pattern – knowledge-grounded, multilingual copilots delivered
where people already are – is a proven delivery layer for NAISH health and
agriculture use cases.

Reference: [gooey.ai](https://gooey.ai/)

<Callout type="info">
  **WhatsApp-first, measurable, open.** Copilots answer from a vetted knowledge
  base with page-level citations, support voice notes and image input for
  low-literacy users, and track adoption/outcomes with "golden" evaluation
  datasets. Core components are open source (Apache-2.0):
  [github.com/GooeyAI](https://github.com/GooeyAI).
</Callout>

## Farmer Chat

**Farmer.CHAT** is a conversational AI **agricultural-advisory copilot** giving
smallholder farmers vetted, locally relevant guidance on planting, irrigation,
pest management, fertilisation, and climate risk. It is trained **exclusively on
trusted local resources** – farmer-to-farmer videos, government extension
documents, and best-practice manuals – and built by **Gooey.AI with
[Digital Green](https://www.digitalgreen.org)**. It is multimodal (text, audio,
image) and multilingual (Hindi, Telugu, Bhojpuri, Amharic, Swahili, English),
delivered over WhatsApp, Telegram, and web.

## UN IOM – Afiya

**Afiya** is an **AI health agent on WhatsApp** created by the **UN
International Organization for Migration (IOM)** office in Tunisia and powered by
Gooey.AI. It serves migrants in Tunisia and neighbouring countries with health
information (vaccination, pregnancy, mental health) and service navigation.
WhatsApp was chosen because migrants often lack a local SIM card.

Features: 24/7 multilingual support (20+ languages incl. regional dialects),
voice messaging for low-literacy users, structured care-pathway guidance, and an
integrated service locator of **2,000+ GPS-tagged health facilities** linked to
Google Maps.

## Relevance to NAISH

- **WhatsApp-first, multilingual delivery** matches how Nigerian frontline
  workers and the public already communicate – the exact pattern of Afiya
  (Tunisia), Medtronic Labs (Bangladesh), and Medic (Kenya), extensible to
  Hausa, Yoruba, Igbo, and Pidgin with voice for low-literacy users.
- **Knowledge-grounded, cited answers** support clinical safety and trust for
  health advisory.
- **Referral & service-locator pattern** (Afiya's 2,000+ facilities, 1,707
  referrals) is a transferable model for connecting patients to Nigerian primary
  health centres.
- **Open-source, self-hostable components** (Apache-2.0 server + embeddable
  widget) support data sovereignty and local hosting on NAISH infrastructure.
- **Measurability** aligns with the M&E requirements of Gates-funded programs.
