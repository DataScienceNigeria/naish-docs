**AfricanVoices** is DSN's platform for **democratizing African speech data for
research and innovation**. It bridges the data gap for underrepresented African
languages in AI by providing curated, richly-labelled speech datasets that
researchers and developers can search, filter, and download. It is live at
[africanvoices.io](https://africanvoices.io/), **managed by Data Science Nigeria
and supported by the Gates Foundation**, and it is the direct data foundation for
the Hub's [Voice & Local-Language Datasets](/docs/capabilities/voice-language-datasets)
capability.

## Mission

To advance **inclusive AI** by making high-quality African speech data
accessible – closing the gap that leaves most Nigerian and African languages
under-served by mainstream voice technology.

Languages covered: **Hausa, Igbo, Nigerian Pidgin, and Yoruba.**

## What it provides

- **Curated multilingual speech datasets** with metadata (age, gender, domain).
- **Advanced search & demographic filtering** – find audio by language and
  speaker attributes.
- **Customizable downloads** – take a chunk of a dataset (5–100%) rather than
  all-or-nothing.
- **Community feedback loop** for continuous quality improvement.

## Partners

Beyond DSN and the Gates Foundation, AfricanVoices is delivered with:

- **Awarri Technologies**

- **University of Ibadan – Department of Linguistics**
- **RobotsMali**

## Who it's for

Researchers, developers, and students building **voice-AI applications for
African languages** – ASR, TTS, and multilingual conversational systems.

## Relevance to NAISH

AfricanVoices supplies exactly the data several NAISH use cases depend on. The
[Agricultural Advisory](/docs/use-cases) chatbot (Hausa / Yoruba / Igbo) and the
[Oral Reading Fluency](/docs/use-cases) assessment (local-language ASR) both need
large, curated Nigerian-language speech corpora – precisely what AfricanVoices
curates. It is the concrete dataset layer behind the Hub's
[voice & local-language](/docs/capabilities/voice-language-datasets) capability,
and a reason [data readiness](/docs/capabilities/data-readiness) for voice use
cases is further along than it would otherwise be.
